The Cybersecurity Factory

A Summer Program for Security Companies

Early stage security companies currently face many obstacles. First of all, security solutions often need to be deeply technical yet accessible to customers. Security companies need to instill trust into their customers, which makes it difficult for new companies to enter into the market. Consumer and enterprise sales are especially challenging. The Security Factory is our solution.

The Cybersecurity Factory is an 8-week summer program that gives teams:

$20,000 Investment

Generous terms as a convertible note

Office Space

In Kendall Square's startup hotbed

Mentorship

Seasoned technical and business mentors to help transform your technical idea into a commercial solution

Network

A network of entrepreneurs and investors interested in security companies

We will run our pilot program in Cambridge, MA this summer with 3-4 teams. We are looking for early-stage technical startups, preferably with at least one co-founder who is currently in a PhD program or has completed a PhD in a topic related to the startup focus.

Application deadline: mid-April

Summer program: mid-June to mid-August

Our application isn't up yet, but you can sign up to receive updates.

If there any questions not answered in the FAQ, feel free to contact us at cybersecurityfactory@mit.edu.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the Cybersecurity Factory?
A: A 8-week summer program for early-stage security startups. We will provide access to mentors, leadership development programming for the founding team, access to potential customers, and a community for providing feedback on both the technical solution and the consumer-facing product.
Q: What is the scope of our pilot program?
A: We will fund and mentor 3-4 teams in Cambridge, MA during summer 2015. We will provide programming to help the teams build their brands and we will organize a “demo day” at the end to allow the teams to show off their progress in their work and potentially raise the next round of funding.
Q: What teams are eligible?
A: Any team can apply. We will give preference to teams with 2-3 members where one is a current or recent PhD student with an area of study related to the technical focus of the company.
Q: What is the structure of the program?
A: The goal is to provide sufficient support to be beneficial to teams without getting in the way. There will be regular check-ins with the other teams and weekly check-ins with mentors. The Cybersecurity Factory team will be available for workshopping both the technical and business aspects of the product. There will be a close affiliation with a Cambridge venture capital firm (to be announced).
Q: What do teams get out of this?
A: Besides getting access to money and office space, teams will benefit from the following:
  • Environment for technical feedback and support.
  • Network of investors and potential customers.
  • Business and technical mentoring by investors and academic advisors.
  • Community of founders interested in technical security problems.
Q: What do we expect teams to achieve by the end of the summer?
A: A technical artifact validated by academic mentor, a customer demo, and a honed customer pitch of the product. A stretch milestone is to raise seed funding within three months of leaving the Security Factory.
Q: What happens after my application is submitted? How will it take to hear back from you?
A: We plan to have a quick turnaround. Your application will be reviewed by at least two people (including one of the core team) before we decide to pass on it or move forward. Once we make a decision, we will reach out. If your team needs quicker turnaround, please email us at cybersecurityfactory@mit.edu.
Q: What happens after the summer?
A: The goal of the summer camp is to provide better guidance on your security product. During the summer, we will connect you with various VCs as well as customers so that you can have more complete product. We hope that our companies continue to work on their product and raise further helping. We will also be in contact to help you with any other needs and problems. Finally, another main goal is to establish a solid alumni network for companies that come of this summer camp in the future.
